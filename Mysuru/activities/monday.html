Planning Your Day in Mysore for Dasara

Here’s a suggested itinerary for your day!

Morning

  • Breakfast: Start your day with a traditional Mysore breakfast at a local eatery. Try dishes like Mysore Masala Dosa or Idli-Vada with coconut chutney.
  • Visit the Mysore Palace: Head to the Mysore Palace early to avoid the crowds. The palace is beautifully illuminated during Dasara, but visiting in the morning allows you to explore its stunning architecture and history.

Mid-Morning

  • St. Philomena's Church: After the palace, visit St. Philomena's Church, one of the largest churches in India. Its neo-gothic architecture is quite impressive.

Lunch

  • Local Cuisine: Enjoy lunch at a restaurant offering local Mysore cuisine. RRR or Mylari are popular options for traditional meals.

Afternoon

  • Dasara Exhibition: Visit the Dasara Exhibition at Doddakere Ground (if it’s happening this year). It showcases local crafts, products, and cultural performances.
  • Chamundi Hill: If you’re up for it, take a trip to Chamundi Hill. You can either drive or hike the 1,000 steps to the temple. The view of the city from the top is breathtaking.

Evening

  • Dasara Procession: Make your way back to the city for the grand Dasara procession, which usually starts around 4 PM. It features beautifully decorated elephants, folk dances, and traditional music. Find a good spot along the procession route to enjoy the festivities.

Dinner

  • Dinner: After the procession, enjoy dinner at a restaurant in Mysore. Hotel RRR or The Old House offer great options for local and continental food.

Night

  • Mysore Palace Illumination: If you have time, check out the palace’s illumination after the procession. It’s a spectacular sight with thousands of lights adorning the structure.

Tips

Transportation: Consider using local transport or rickshaws to get around, as parking might be challenging during Dasara.

Stay Hydrated: October can be warm, so carry water and stay hydrated.

Dress Comfortably: Wear comfortable clothing and shoes, especially if you plan to do a lot of walking.

Enjoy your Dasara celebrations in Mysore!
