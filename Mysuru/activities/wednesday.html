Mysore Dasara Itinerary

Morning

6:30 AM – Chamundi Hill

Start your day early with a visit to Chamundi Hill. You can hike the 1,000 steps or take a vehicle up to the Chamundeshwari Temple. The temple will be vibrant during Dasara, and the early morning is ideal to avoid crowds. The sunrise view of the city from the top is breathtaking.

8:30 AM – Breakfast at Mylari

Head to Hotel Mylari for a classic Mysore breakfast. Their signature dish is the Benne Dosa (butter dosa), known for its softness and flavor, paired with coconut chutney.

Mid-Morning

10:00 AM – Mysore Palace

Explore the Mysore Palace, which is beautifully adorned during the Dasara festivities. The palace opens early, so you’ll have plenty of time to tour the interior, admire the architecture, and learn about its royal history. During Dasara, the palace is especially lively with decorations and events.

Late Morning

11:30 AM – St. Philomena’s Church

After the palace, visit St. Philomena’s Church, one of the largest churches in India. Its neo-gothic architecture is stunning and it’s a peaceful spot to admire and relax after your morning tour.

Lunch

1:00 PM – Lunch at RRR or Vinayaka Mylari

Enjoy a delicious South Indian meal at RRR Restaurant or a traditional vegetarian thali at Vinayaka Mylari. Both restaurants are renowned for serving flavorful local dishes.

Afternoon

2:30 PM – Mysore Zoo

Spend a relaxed afternoon at the Mysore Zoo, one of the oldest and most well-maintained zoos in India. It’s a fun and educational experience, especially if you’re interested in wildlife.

4:30 PM – Dasara Exhibition

Head to the Dasara Exhibition at Doddakere Ground. This exhibition offers a wide variety of local crafts, food stalls, and rides. You can also enjoy cultural performances that reflect the local heritage.

Evening

6:30 PM – Dasara Procession

Find a good spot to watch the Dasara Procession, which typically starts in the evening. The parade features caparisoned elephants, folk dancers, musicians, and a rich display of Mysore’s royal history. It’s a highlight of the Dasara celebration and not to be missed.

Dinner

8:00 PM – Dinner at La Uppu (Grand Mercure Mysore)

For dinner, treat yourself to a meal at La Uppu in the Grand Mercure hotel. It offers a mix of international and local flavors in a more upscale setting. Alternatively, you can opt for Hotel RRR if you prefer a more traditional South Indian meal.

Night

9:00 PM – Palace Illumination

Conclude your day with a final look at the Mysore Palace illuminated with thousands of lights. The palace looks magical at night, especially during the Dasara season.
