About Dasara

The Mysore Dasara traces its origins to the Vijayanagara Empire in the 15th century. The festival was later adapted and continued by the Wadiyar dynasty, who ruled the Kingdom of Mysore. According to legend, the celebration is dedicated to the victory of Goddess Chamundeshwari (an incarnation of Durga) over the demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.
